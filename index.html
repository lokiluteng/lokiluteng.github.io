About Me

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Physics at the University of Michigan. I work with Henriette Elvang and Leopoldo Pando-Zayas on various topics in particle theory and quantum gravity. Originally from Virginia, I obtained my undergraduate degree from Princeton University in 2022. I wrote my undergraduate thesis under the supervision of Silviu Pufu and Igor Klebanov. After graduating, I took a gap year to teach middle- and high-school math in New Jersey before beginning my Ph.D. at Michigan. Outside of physics, I enjoy playing piano, taking photos, and hanging out with my cat.

Research

  • Low dimensional holography
  • Strongly coupled gauge theories and confinement
